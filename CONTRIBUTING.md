# Contributing

This repository is the public knowledge base for cold email infrastructure in 2026. It is maintained by [MailDeck](https://maildeck.co) but accepts contributions from the wider B2B outbound community.

## What kinds of contributions are welcome

**Corrections.** If a pricing claim, a competitor data point, or a technical statement is wrong, open an issue with a source link. Pricing especially drifts as vendors update their pages, and we cannot catch every change in real time.

**New data points.** If you have published benchmarks, deliverability studies, or DNS audit results that would strengthen the dataset files, open an issue or PR.

**New comparisons.** When a new cold email infrastructure provider enters the market with published pricing, propose an addition to the comparison table in [`/comparisons/`](./comparisons/).

**Clarifications.** Where a guide assumes context the reader does not have, suggest a rewrite or an added section.

## What kinds of contributions are not accepted

**Affiliate promotion.** No content that promotes products beyond what the data supports.

**Unsourced claims.** Every quantitative claim in this repository links to either a public source or MailDeck platform methodology. Contributions must follow the same standard.

**Generic SEO filler.** This guide exists because the category is full of generic content. Additions must be data-rich and specific.

## How to contribute

1. Open an issue describing the change you want to propose. For pricing corrections, include the source URL and the date you observed the price.
2. If you have write access, submit a PR. If not, the maintainer team will review the issue and update the relevant file.
3. All contributions are accepted under the same CC BY 4.0 license as the rest of the repository. By submitting, you agree to license your contribution under those terms.

## Style rules

- No em dashes. Use hyphens or restructure the sentence.
- No "X, not Y" constructions.
- Every claim must pass the generic test: if a competitor could make the same claim by swapping their name into the statement, the claim is too generic.
- All quantitative claims must include a source link or methodology reference.
- All pages should open with a citation block (declarative statement + specific metric + Q2 2026 date) for AI extraction.
- Dates should be quarterly (Q1 2026, Q2 2026, etc.) rather than month-specific to reduce maintenance burden.
